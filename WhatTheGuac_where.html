Avocados Originated in Mexico and Central America!

Avocados are believed to have originated in the state of Puebla in Mexico. The earliest evidence of avocado consumption dates back to around 10,000 BC, when they were cultivated by the indigenous people of Mexico, Central and South America. The name "avocado" comes from the Aztec word "ahuacatl," which means "testicle," a reference to the fruit's shape. Today, avocados are grown in many parts of the world, including Australia, Brazil, California, Colombia, Chile, China, Dominican Republic, Florida, Guatemala, Haiti, Indonesia, Israel, Jamaica, Kenya, New Zealand, Peru, South Africa, Spain, Tanzania, and Zimbabwe.

Avocados Are an Ancient Fruit!

The avocado, also known as "aguacate" in Spanish, has a long history in Mexico, particularly in the state of Puebla. The ancient Mesoamerican civilizations of Mexico, including the Aztecs, Mayas, and Olmecs, all cultivated and consumed avocados. Archaeological evidence suggests that avocados were present in Puebla as early as 10,000 BC.
In pre-Columbian times, avocados were considered a symbol of fertility and were often used in religious ceremonies. They were also believed to have medicinal properties and were used to treat a variety of ailments, including skin conditions, digestive problems, and respiratory issues.

The cultivation of avocados in Puebla and other parts of Mexico was passed down from generation to generation, and the techniques used for growing avocados have been refined over thousands of years. Today, Puebla remains a major producer of avocados, with many small-scale farmers growing the fruit using traditional methods.

The avocado has become a popular food around the world, with demand for the fruit increasing in recent years due to its nutritional value and versatility in cooking. However, the avocado remains an important part of Mexican culture and cuisine, and is still celebrated in festivals and ceremonies throughout the country.

There are actually many different types of avocados!

The exact number of avocado varieties is not known precisely, however, it is estimated that there are over 1,000 different varieties in the world. Each variety has its own unique flavor and texture, and appearance. Here are the top 15 most popular avocado varieties:

1. Hass - The most popular avocado variety, known for its creamy texture, rich and nutty flavor.
2. Fuerte - A thinner-skinned avocado with a smooth texture and nutty flavor.
3. Bacon - A small to medium-sized avocado with a buttery, nutty flavor.
4. Zutano - A mild-flavored avocado with a smooth texture and green skin.
5. Pinkerton - A large, pear-shaped avocado with a smooth, creamy texture and a nutty flavor.
6. Reed - A large, round avocado with a buttery, creamy texture and a mild flavor.
7. Lamb Hass - A Hass avocado crossbred with a Gwen avocado, known for its buttery flavor and creamy texture.
8. Gwen - A larger avocado with a creamy, nutty flavor and a smooth texture.
9. Sir Prize - A newer variety of avocado with a high oil content, creamy texture, and buttery flavor.
10. Sharwil - A Hawaiian variety of avocado with a rich, nutty flavor and a creamy texture.
11. Ettinger - A mild-flavored avocado with a smooth texture and thin skin.
12. Choquette - A Florida-grown variety with a buttery texture and a mild, nutty flavor.
13. Lula - A Florida-grown avocado with a creamy texture and a mild, buttery flavor.
14. Carmen - A Mexican variety with a smooth texture and a buttery, nutty flavor.
15. Maluma - A newer variety of avocado with a high oil content, creamy texture, and a sweet, nutty flavor.
